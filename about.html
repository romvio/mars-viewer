About the Atlas

The Atlas of Mars is a visual and scientific record of our neighboring planet, created entirely with images captured by the Emirates Mars Mission (EMM), also known as Hope (Al-Amal). This groundbreaking mission, launched on July 20, 2020, marked the Arab world's first interplanetary exploration.

Hope entered Mars orbit on February 9, 2021, and is equipped with advanced instruments that allow for unprecedented observation of the Martian atmosphere and surface. Its unique orbit enables it to scan the entire planet every 9–10 days, providing rich, full-disk views used in this atlas.

This web-based interactive version of the Atlas has been developed by the NYU Abu Dhabi Center for Space Science as part of the PPTP initiative. It brings together over 580 images captured by the Emirates eXploration Imager (EXI) between February and August 2021, stitched together using advanced mapping techniques including cylindrical and Robinson projections.

The atlas showcases labeled regions, major geological features, and stunning high-resolution photographs of Mars, some of which are being made publicly accessible for the first time. Our goal is to make Mars exploration intuitive, educational, and visually captivating.

This site will continue to evolve as the Hope mission provides more data. Stay curious — Mars still has many secrets waiting to be revealed.

For more information, please visit our Mars Atlas Website.
